About the game

source: Steam store page

Master the art of starfighter combat in the authentic piloting experience STAR WARS™: Squadrons. Buckle up and feel the adrenaline of first-person, multiplayer space dogfights alongside your squadron. Pilots who enlist will step into the cockpits of starfighters from both the New Republic and Imperial fleets and fight in strategic 5v5 space battles. Modify your starfighter, and adjust the composition of your squadron to suit varying playstyles and crush the opposition.

Pilots will triumph as a team and complete tactical objectives across known and never-before-seen battlefields, including the gas giant of Yavin Prime and the shattered moon of Galitan. Take control of starfighters such as the X-wing and TIE fighter. Customize loadouts and cosmetics. Divert power between weapons, shields, and engines while immersing yourself in the cockpit. In addition, players will have the option to play the entirety of the game in virtual reality (VR)!

The cockpit is your home. Use its dashboards to your advantage and — with just a thin hull of metal and glass between you and the perils of space — feel the intensity of combat from a first-person perspective.

Learn what it means to be a pilot after the events of Return of the Jedi. Seen from alternating perspectives between 2 factions, iconic and emerging leaders on both sides are battling for the galaxy. The New Republic fights for freedom. The Empire demands order. We need you to join the galaxy’s finest.

Take control of different classes of starfighters from both the New Republic and Imperial fleets — including the agile A-wing and the devastating TIE bomber.

Choose who to fight for

Who will you fight for? The Empire? The Rebel Alliance? Make your choice; and when you choose, just remember one thing.

The Death Star was a massive orbital battle station the size of a moon. Constructued by the Galactic Empire, this battle station cost over 1 trillion Galactic credits and carried a crew of 1,206,293 personnel, 342,953 Imperial Navy and Army garrisons, 25,984 Stormtroopers, and an additional 750,000 passengers. Totaling 2,325,230 people.

The Death Star II was in the midst of construction by the Galactic Empire after the destruction of the first Death Star. This new battle station was significantly larger that its predecessor. It carried a crew of 485,560 personnel, 152,275 troopers, 127,570 infantry, 75,860 technical personnel, and 334,432 pilots.
Totaling 2,471,647 people.

During the Battle of Yavin in the year 0 BBY (Before Battle of Yavin) Rebel forces launched an attack on the Death Star orbital battle station. This attack resulted in the destruction of the Death Star and the deaths of all 2,325,230 people on board the orbital battle station.
During the Battle of Endor in the year 4 ABY (After the Battle of Yavin) Rebel forces launched another attack. This time against the Death Star II. This attack resulted in the destruction of the Death Star II, the start of the Imperial Civil War, and the deaths of all 2,471,647 people on board the battle station.

4,796,877
The collective death toll of both the Battle of Yavin and the Battle of Endor.
These victims were simply trying to earn credits to feed and support their families on distant planets. They were looking forward to seeing them again in the future after being so far away for so long. These victims had children, spouses, parents, and grandchildren waiting their return from duty. While the Rebels cheered and celebrated the destructions of the Death Star and Death Star II; across the galaxy the families of 4,796,877 people were mourning the loss of their loved ones.

Just like a Factor 5 game

In December 18, 2000, Factor 5 released a game titled Star Wars Episode I: Battle for Naboo. Prior to this game they had also developed and released "Rogue Squadron" but Battle for Naboo perfected the formula they had created for Rogue Squadron. Battle for Naboo had exciting gameplay, impressive graphics and what many described as "tight and responsive" controls.
Squadrons feels just like going back and playing a Factor 5 game all over again. Great graphics, great gameplay, and great controls.
