Changing Video Games

While not the first, the original Doom released in 1993 brought much wider popularity to the first person shooter genre of video games and is considered the most influential game in this genre. For many years, "Doom clone" was used as the name for this genre due to it's influence.

idspispopd easter egg

The cheat code "idspispopd" from the original doom, particularly "SPISPOPD", can be traced back to a post by Eli S. Bingham in December, 1993.

id Software later gave a nod to this joke as a cheat code in the game. The band "The Smashing Pumpkins" also referenced the joke by having Doom samples in their song "Where Boys Fear to Tread".

Later Two computer games were created based on the joke.

Smashing Pumpkins into Small Piles of Putrid Debris is a 2D action game from Jamul Software (later Hamumu Software) in which the player collects candles and smashes pumpkins with a red hammer. It was reportedly developed in 48 hours to capitalize on the joke.

Hamumu Software later created a similar game called Amazin' SPISPOPD, and several other games based off of its rendition of SPISPOPD that did not share any similarities to the original fictional title.

Replayability

The original Doom and Doom 2 had and has a map builder that allows players to create custom and unique content.
A lot of amazing levels or "wads" have been created and continue to be created to this day. (ever since 1993!) Some teams of mappers or even extraordinarily talented solo mappers have created what are known as "mega wads". These mega wads consist of 32 levels and are essentially an entirely new game in the doom engine. Many different mega wads exist and can still be played today with the original Doom in a DOS emulator like DOSbox or more conveniently on modern source ports of the Doom engine code such as Boom, Chocolate Doom, Crispy Doom, GZDoom, and many more.

These modern source ports have many different features. For example, keeping true to the original Doom bugs and all but making it run on modern systems without the use of DOSbox; while some other source ports fix those bugs. However many people choose to play with the bugs in place for a more authentic and "true" experience. Further still, another source port, GZDoom makes use of modern graphics libraries to bring 3D to Doom and Doom 2's textures and even allows the player to look up and down without any distortion or stretched textures.

Other talented members of the community have also performed magic, or "skillful programming", to bring not just 3D visuals to Doom but also fully 3D character models and have created their own 3D games within Doom.

It is because of these amazing community maps, sourceports, mods, and new creations that Doom and Doom 2 are still played and continue to have a thriving community almost 3 Decades after Doom's initial release.
