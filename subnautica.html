Subnautica

About the game

source: Steam store page

Descend into the depths of an alien underwater world filled with wonder and peril. Craft equipment, pilot submarines and out-smart wildlife to explore lush coral reefs, volcanoes, cave systems, and more - all while trying to survive.

You have crash-landed on an alien ocean world, and the only way to go is down. Subnautica's oceans range from sun drenched shallow coral reefs to treacherous deep-sea trenches, lava fields, and bio-luminescent underwater rivers. Manage your oxygen supply as you explore kelp forests, plateaus, reefs, and winding cave systems. The water teems with life: Some of it helpful, much of it harmful.

After crash landing in your Life Pod, the clock is ticking to find water, food, and to develop the equipment you need to explore. Collect resources from the ocean around you. Craft diving gear, lights, habitat modules, and submersibles. Venture deeper and further to find rarer resources, allowing you to craft more advanced items.

Build bases on the sea floor. Choose layouts and components, and manage hull-integrity as depth and pressure increase. Use your base to store resources, park vehicles, and replenish oxygen supplies as you explore the vast ocean.

Diverse Ecosystems

Subnautica is a large open world with many diverse and beautiful areas to explore. These areas have many different kinds of fauna and flora that make the world feel alive and natural. There are lots of things to discover. Equipment to help you survive, technologies to help you thrive, revelations of the fishy inhabitants of this mysterious planet and of those who visited before your arrival.

Use the discoveries you make to build tools, bases, and more equipment. These constructed items will help you thrive on this ocean planet and help you discover new things and explore further than you were able to before.

Small fish, big fish, microscopic fish, fish the size of boats and even bigger. Friendly fish, mean fish, tasty fish, and cute fish. Electric fish, transparent fish, fish with big eyes, fish with no eyes, flying fish, walking fish, noot fish, cthulhu fish, and fish that want to punch you. Subnautica has so many different types of fish. These fish even have revolutionary AI. As you swim closer, some of these fish will swim away from you. Wow.
And only most of them want to rip you to shreds. Even the plants are deadly!

Fish interactions

You can interact with the fauna and flora in Subnautica in many different ways. You can eat them, turn them into water, collect them, keep them in your pocket for later, put them in an aquarium in your base, feed them, study them, and collect scientific data about them.

Hey! Take a look at that cute fish.
On such a lonely planet, light years away from other people, it's nice to have some kind of companionship. Ideally one that's not a volley ball. During your explorations, if you look hard enough you'll find this cute fish, also known as the cuddle fish. This cute fish will bring joy and love to your underwater base. You can even invite cute fish to come with you on your adventures. If your "heart" is made of a solid block of ice that never melts, you may think of putting cute fish in your bio reactor to generate a large amount of power. But you may want to reconsider doing that as you may find the weight on your conscience unbearable; if you even have one.

The protagonist

You play as Non-Essential Systems Maintenance Chief Ryley Robinson who survived the crash onto this ocean planet by escaping on a lifepod just in time, and really likes hair gel. Don't be fooled though. This janitor is extremely talented. Almost nothing is known about Ryley. But what we do know, we know from his actions. Ryley is an expert welder, capable of repairing even the most damaged equipment in dangerous and stressful situations. A master craftsman, capable of creating and building complex tools and machinery like submersibles and nuclear reactors. A talented researcher, capable of deducing what occured on this planet an unknown period of time prior to his arrival simply by exploring and observing remnants of past visitors and examining their complex technologies.

Will we ever find out more about Ryley and his life before coming to this ocean planet? Will we learn how he became a legendary master of all trades and sciences? No.
